Research Projects

Audio JEPA for Deepfake Detection

2024-2025 | Self-Supervised Learning, Synthetic Media Detection

Developed a novel self-supervised learning approach for audio deepfake detection using Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA). The system employs a two-stage training paradigm: first learning representations from real audio only, then fine-tuning a classifier on both real and fake samples.

Key Contributions:

  • Implemented AudioJEPAEncoder and Predictor architectures for temporal audio modeling
  • Achieved 5-10% improvement in out-of-distribution generalization over supervised baselines
  • Integrated SNGP (Spectral Normalized Gaussian Process) for uncertainty quantification
  • Released open-source codebase with 12,000+ lines of production-ready code

Technologies: PyTorch, VICReg Loss, Mel-Spectrograms, Uncertainty Quantification

Robustness Under Distribution Shift and Adversarial Attacks

2023-2024 | AI Assurance, Adversarial Machine Learning

Developed methods for achieving simultaneous robustness under both distribution shifts and adversarial attacks, addressing a critical gap in trustworthy AI systems. Published at AAAI.

Key Contributions:

  • Novel training methodology combining robustness to natural and adversarial perturbations
  • Theoretical analysis of trade-offs between different robustness objectives
  • Empirical validation on multiple benchmark datasets and shift scenarios
  • Published findings at the AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence

Underconfidence Attack Detection and Mitigation

2022-2023 | Adversarial Machine Learning, Model Security

Investigated underconfidence vulnerabilities in machine learning models where adversarial examples cause models to be overly uncertain about correct predictions. Developed detection and mitigation strategies to address this emerging threat.

Key Contributions:

  • Characterized underconfidence attack mechanisms and their impact on model reliability
  • Developed detection methods to identify when models are under attack
  • Proposed defense strategies to maintain model confidence under adversarial conditions
  • Published research findings in peer-reviewed venue

Research Interests

My research interests span several interconnected areas in trustworthy and robust AI:

  • AI Assurance: Building reliable and trustworthy AI systems with formal guarantees
  • Adversarial Machine Learning: Understanding and defending against adversarial attacks
  • Self-Supervised Learning: Learning robust representations without manual labels
  • Deepfake Detection: Detecting AI-generated synthetic media (audio, video, images)
  • Uncertainty Quantification: Building AI systems that know when they don't know
  • OOD Generalization: Ensuring models work reliably on unseen data distributions
