About Me

I recently completed my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Connecticut, where I focused on AI Assurance and Adversarial Machine Learning. My work explores robust and trustworthy AI systems, with particular emphasis on ensuring reliability under distribution shifts and adversarial perturbations.

During my doctoral research, I developed novel approaches for detecting underconfidence in machine learning models and achieving robustness against both adversarial attacks and distribution shifts. My work has been published in top-tier venues including AAAI, and I've contributed to advancing the field of AI safety and security.

I'm passionate about building trustworthy AI systems, detecting synthetic media (deepfakes), and advancing self-supervised learning. I'm currently seeking opportunities for research collaborations and positions in AI assurance, adversarial robustness, and synthetic media detection.
