About Me

I recently completed my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Connecticut under the supervision of Dr. Sheida Nabavi and Dr. Derek Aguiar, in collaboration with MIT Lincoln Laboratory with Olivia Brown as my mentor. My research focused on AI Assurance and Adversarial Machine Learning, exploring robust and trustworthy AI systems with particular emphasis on ensuring reliability under distribution shifts and adversarial perturbations.

During my doctoral research at MIT Lincoln Laboratory (May 2022 – December 2025), I led the development of synthetic media detection pipelines achieving 95–99% AUC on benchmarks. I developed novel approaches for detecting underconfidence in machine learning models and improving robustness against adversarial attacks, resulting in publications at leading venues including AAAI, NeurIPS, and NLDL, with work demonstrating 20% improvement over state-of-the-art methods.

I'm passionate about building trustworthy AI systems, detecting synthetic media (deepfakes), and advancing self-supervised learning. I'm currently seeking opportunities for research collaborations and positions in AI assurance, adversarial robustness, and synthetic media detection.
