Research Projects

Underconfidence Adversarial Training

Addressed a critical but overlooked vulnerability in adversarially trained models: attacks that reduce model confidence without changing predictions. Decreased confidence can result in unnecessary interventions, delayed diagnoses, and erosion of trust in automated systems—particularly problematic in high-stakes domains like medical imaging and autonomous systems.

Key Contributions:

  • Introduced two novel underconfidence attack methods: class-pair ambiguity attacks and ConfSmooth, which spreads uncertainty across all classes
  • Developed Underconfidence Adversarial Training (UAT), a defense framework that integrates these attacks into standard adversarial training
  • Demonstrated UAT achieves comparable or better robustness than traditional adversarial training while requiring only half the gradient steps
  • Comprehensive evaluation across 6 architectures (CNNs and Vision Transformers) and 7 datasets including MNIST, CIFAR, ImageNet, MSTAR, and medical imaging

Impact: Expanded adversarial robustness considerations beyond misclassification to encompass uncertainty-aware threats, addressing a critical gap in current defense mechanisms.

Model-Based Robust Training: From Snow to Rain

Investigated how deep learning models handle natural image corruptions like snow and rain in safety-critical applications. Developed and evaluated training methods using learned corruption models to generate realistic distortions, with a focus on balancing robustness, calibration, and computational efficiency.

Key Contributions:

  • Comparative framework evaluating model-based training approaches against baselines (Vanilla, Adversarial Training, AugMix) across varying corruption severity levels
  • Introduced hybrid strategies combining random coverage with adversarial refinement in nuisance space to balance performance and efficiency
  • Multi-dimensional analysis assessing accuracy, calibration quality, and computational requirements—moving beyond accuracy-only metrics
  • Demonstrated that model-based data augmentation delivers comparable robustness to adversarial approaches with significantly lower computational demands

Key Findings: Learned corruption models effectively capture natural variability, offering a promising direction toward more resilient and well-calibrated systems under adverse conditions.

Robust Training for Medical Imaging Classification

Developed robust training strategies to strengthen deep neural networks used in medical image diagnosis against adversarial attacks and distribution shifts while maintaining diagnostic accuracy. Critical for ensuring model reliability and maintaining clinician trust when facing adversarial examples or real-world data variations.

Key Contributions:

  • Proposed Robust Training with Data Augmentation (RTDA), combining robust training strategies with data augmentation techniques specifically designed for medical imaging
  • Comprehensive benchmarking across 6 competing baseline approaches, including isolated and combined adversarial training and augmentation methods
  • Multi-modal validation across three distinct imaging technologies: mammograms, X-rays, and ultrasound, demonstrating broad applicability
  • Evaluated robustness against both adversarial perturbations and natural distribution variations while maintaining clean accuracy

Impact: Addresses critical healthcare concerns around maintaining model reliability in clinical settings where adversarial attacks or data distribution shifts could compromise patient safety.

Research Interests

My research interests span several interconnected areas in trustworthy and robust AI:

  • AI Assurance: Building reliable and trustworthy AI systems with formal guarantees
  • Adversarial Machine Learning: Understanding and defending against adversarial attacks
  • Self-Supervised Learning: Learning robust representations without manual labels
  • Deepfake Detection: Detecting AI-generated synthetic media (audio, video, images)
  • Uncertainty Quantification: Building AI systems that know when they don't know
  • OOD Generalization: Ensuring models work reliably on unseen data distributions
