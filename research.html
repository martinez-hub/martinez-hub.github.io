Audio JEPA for Deepfake Detection

2024-2025 | Self-Supervised Learning, Synthetic Media Detection

Developed a novel self-supervised learning approach for audio deepfake detection using Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA). The system employs a two-stage training paradigm: first learning representations from real audio only, then fine-tuning a classifier on both real and fake samples.

Key Contributions:

  • Implemented AudioJEPAEncoder and Predictor architectures for temporal audio modeling
  • Achieved 5-10% improvement in out-of-distribution generalization over supervised baselines
  • Integrated SNGP (Spectral Normalized Gaussian Process) for uncertainty quantification
  • Released open-source codebase with 12,000+ lines of production-ready code

Technologies: PyTorch, VICReg Loss, Mel-Spectrograms, Uncertainty Quantification
